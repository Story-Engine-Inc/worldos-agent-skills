# Simulation review rubric

## Blocking

A finding is blocking when the Simulation cannot be meaningfully played or trusted:

- no usable player-action surface;
- the opening cannot establish a valid player identity;
- required characters or app references are missing;
- core state repeatedly disappears or contradicts itself;
- a required app is absent or unusable;
- the player is routinely deprived of agency;
- sensitive internal instructions are shown to the player.

## Significant

A finding is significant when play continues but the intended experience is materially weakened:

- characters ignore direct communication;
- objectives do not progress after established requirements are met;
- outcomes are granted without preparation or resistance;
- time, travel, recovery, or deadlines become implausible;
- prose and visible app state disagree;
- too many apps own the same fact;
- map ownership, faction identity, or location becomes inconsistent;
- locale fallback is frequent or confusing.

## Polish

A finding is polish when mechanics remain coherent:

- repetitive phrasing;
- weak opening suggestions despite a usable input surface;
- minor label or ordering problems;
- excessive but harmless seeded content;
- one-off voice inconsistency;
- nonessential visual density.

## Evidence standards

Use “confirmed” only when the save state or turn sequence directly demonstrates the claim. Use “likely” when multiple observations support an explanation but the exact cause is not visible. Use “possible” for a hypothesis that needs a new controlled run.

Avoid diagnosing from one surprising result. Look for:

- a stated fact or successful consequence;
- the app or world area responsible for it;
- a later turn that should preserve or use it;
- a contradiction, disappearance, or invalid reference.

## Verification plan

Recommend a fresh-save scenario that is short and falsifiable:

1. start with one known setup;
2. take one action that should alter the target fact;
3. observe the immediate prose and visible app state;
4. advance one or two unrelated turns;
5. verify the fact remains and affects a later response.

Do not prescribe editing an existing save as a verification method.
